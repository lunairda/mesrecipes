steps:
  - "Make the filling: mash the cooked red beans in a saucepan with a masher or fork (or blend for a smoother texture). Add the brown sugar, cinnamon, vanilla extract, ginger and salt. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until you have a thick, shapeable paste. If it feels too dry, add a splash of water at a time."
  - "Cool and wrap the strawberries: spread the paste on a plate and let it cool until just warm, then divide into 8 equal portions. Flatten each portion in your palm, place a whole strawberry in the centre, and wrap the paste fully around it to form a ball. Chill in the fridge until firm."
  - "Make the mochi dough: in a microwave-safe bowl, whisk together the glutinous rice flour, cornstarch, sugar and water until smooth. Microwave in 30–60 second bursts, stirring between each, until the dough turns translucent and stretchy (2–3 minutes total). You can also steam it instead."
  - "Wrap and seal: dust your work surface generously with cornstarch. Divide the hot dough into 8 pieces. Flatten each piece in your hand, place a chilled bean-strawberry ball in the centre, gather the edges up and pinch to seal at the bottom. Dust off any excess cornstarch."
  - "Serve: sprinkle with a little orange zest if you like. Let rest for 10–15 minutes and serve at room temperature. Best enjoyed fresh, the same day."
---

For a lighter version, you can reduce the brown sugar to 30g — the strawberry and beans carry plenty of sweetness on their own. For a chunkier filling instead of smooth, mash only half the beans and leave the rest whole. For a pink hue, try mixing a pinch of freeze-dried strawberry powder into the dusting cornstarch. Mochi firms up in the fridge, so it's best eaten the same day.
