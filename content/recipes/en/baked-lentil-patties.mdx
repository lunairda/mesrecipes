steps:
  - "Cook the lentils in plenty of unsalted water for 20–25 minutes until tender but not mushy. Drain well and let them cool for 10 minutes."
  - "While the lentils cool, grate the courgette and place it in a clean kitchen towel. Squeeze out as much water as possible — this step is important or the patties will be too wet."
  - "Preheat your oven to 200°C (180°C fan). Lightly brush a baking tray with the olive oil."
  - "In a large bowl, roughly mash the lentils with a fork — you want a mix of whole and mashed lentils for a good texture, not a smooth paste."
  - "Add the squeezed courgette, garlic, egg, flaxseed, flour, parsley, dill, cumin, chilli flakes, salt and pepper. Mix well until everything comes together."
  - "If the mixture feels too wet, add a little more flour one tablespoon at a time. It should hold its shape when pressed."
  - "Shape into 10–12 small patties and place on the prepared tray. Press them down gently so they're about 1.5cm thick — this helps them cook evenly."
  - "Bake for 22–25 minutes, flipping halfway, until golden and firm on both sides."
  - "Serve warm with a side salad or a dollop of yoghurt."

These came together on a weeknight when I wanted something protein-rich but light. The trick is really squeezing the courgette dry — skip that step and the patties won't hold together. Everything else is very forgiving.

They're great with a fresh salad alongside, or with a simple yoghurt dip. Leftovers reheat well in a pan the next day too.
