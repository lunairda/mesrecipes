steps:
  - "Make and blind-bake the pastry: mix the whole wheat flour, salt, olive oil and cold water in a bowl until it just comes together — add water a little at a time if needed. Press into a 24cm tart tin, covering the base and sides evenly. Prick the base all over with a fork and blind-bake at 190°C for 12–15 minutes until lightly set."
  - "Blanch the broccoli and cauliflower: cook the florets in boiling salted water for 4–5 minutes until just tender. Drain well and set aside."
  - "Sauté the vegetables: cook the garlic in olive oil over medium heat for 1 minute. Add the courgette rounds and sauté for 3–4 minutes until lightly golden. Add the broccoli and cauliflower, season with salt and pepper, toss for 2 more minutes and remove from the heat."
  - "Make the filling: whisk together the eggs, strained yogurt, grated cheese and nutmeg if using. Season lightly with salt and pepper."
  - "Assemble and bake: spread the sautéed vegetables evenly over the blind-baked pastry. Pour the egg mixture over the top. Bake at 190°C for 25–30 minutes until golden on top and just set in the middle."
  - "Rest and serve: leave to rest for at least 10 minutes before slicing — this makes it much easier to cut cleanly. Serve warm or at room temperature."
---

The key is draining the broccoli and cauliflower well after blanching — soggy vegetables will make the filling watery. Slice the courgette about 3mm thick so it cooks through evenly. For even less fat, halve the cheese and add more seasoning instead. You can swap the strained yogurt for low-fat milk — the sauce will be lighter but slightly less creamy. Tastes great cold too, even for breakfast the next day.
